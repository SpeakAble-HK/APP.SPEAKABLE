# SpeakAble HK · Brand Kit v1

*Final · prepared for the summer 2026 pilot programme*

---

## 0. About this document

This is the working brand kit for SpeakAble HK — the visual and verbal system that governs the web app and its public site. Designed as the working reference for the team building toward the June–August 2026 invite-only pilot.

The brand operates across two surfaces:

| Surface | URL | Audience | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| Public | `speakable.hk` | Outsiders, prospective schools, NGOs, parents | Marketing + demo booking |
| Private | `speakable.naozumi.[domain]` | Pilot participants, internal testers | Logged-in web app |

**The pilot is invite-only through summer 2026.** No public self-serve sign-up exists yet — only demo bookings on the public site. The brand should look launch-ready while the door stays narrow.

---

## 1. Brand essence

### One-line positioning

> An AI-powered assistant tool for everyday Cantonese speech practice — for SEN learners and the people who support them.

### What we are

- An assistant tool that helps people practise Cantonese speaking
- An at-home complement to the work teachers and speech professionals do
- A web app that uses each learner's own cloned voice as the gentlest reference
- A Hong Kong product, made for Hong Kong Cantonese (繁體 + Cantonese vernacular)

### What we are not

- We do not assess, diagnose, or treat
- We are not clinical-grade and do not claim to be
- We do not replace teachers, therapists, or schools
- We are not a game or entertainment app

### Personality pillars

| Pillar | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Warm, not cute | Welcoming to a 6-year-old and a 60-year-old. No baby talk, no sticker overload. |
| Capable, not clinical | AI-powered and honest about it. We earn trust without wearing a lab coat. |
| Patient, not slow | We meet every learner at their pace and celebrate the small wins out loud. |
| Playful, in moderation | Streaks, stars and PiPi's reactions — never confetti for confetti's sake. |

## 2. Audience strategy

The brand has to win three different rooms in three different paragraphs. The funnel is B2B-first, with the consumer experience earned through institutional credibility.

### Tier 1 · Buyers (enters first)

**Schools, NGOs, hospitals**

SEN coordinators, principals, programme directors, department heads. They sign the contracts and renew them.

> "Show me the impact, the reach, and the cost per learner."

### Tier 2 · Champions (enters second)

**Teachers, learning-support staff, programme coordinators**

SEN teachers, classroom assistants, NGO programme coordinators, and the speech professionals who recommend at-home practice. Not our partners — the people in the room with the learner every week.

> "Something easy I can recommend for between sessions — that the family can actually use."

### Tier 3 · Learners (enters third)

**SEN students, adults, families**

The people whose voices we clone. They arrive via a school referral or a parent search — and they meet PiPi here.

> "Will my child be safe, supported, and able to practise at home?"

### Messaging by tier

| Tier | Path | Headline angle | Lead with |
|---|---|---|---|
| Schools & NGOs | `/for-schools` | "Daily Cantonese speech practice, within reach of every learner you support." | Reach, cost per learner, pilot results |
| Educators | `/for-professionals` | "A practice partner that picks up where you leave off." | Workflow fit, progress tracking |
| Families | `/for-families` | "Practice Cantonese at home — in your own voice." | Warmth, PiPi, ease of setup |

## 3. Mascot · PiPi 皮皮

PiPi is the parrot from the master logo. Parrots learn speech by listening, mimicking, refining — exactly what the product asks of every user. PiPi models the behaviour we ask of learners: listen first, then try.

### Where PiPi appears

| Surface | PiPi's role |
|---|---|
| `speakable.hk` homepage | Small cameo near the brand mark; full appearance only in the families section |
| `/for-schools`, `/for-professionals` | Brand mark only — no character illustration |
| `/for-families` | Front and centre |
| Inside the web app | Practice partner — reacts to attempts, celebrates streaks, demonstrates pronunciations |
| Error states | Never — use static logomark only |

### PiPi is

- A tropical parrot with headphones (per the logo)
- Encouraging, slightly cheeky
- Shown reacting *to* the user, not lecturing them
- Useful — celebrating, listening, demonstrating
- Drawn in 2–3 expressions per scene

### PiPi is not

- A teacher or authority figure
- Used for error or failure states
- Made to mimic a real learner's voice in marketing
- Posed with medical props or symbols
- Drawn sad, disappointed, or scolding

### Suggested expression set to commission

`listening` · `demoing` · `celebrating` · `at rest` · `tip mode`

## 4. Color system

A daylight palette pulled from the logo. Sky leads — it carries the brand because it reads as calm, trustworthy and breath-like. Sunshine and Coral handle warmth and celebration. Mint marks progress and growth.

All combinations below tested at WCAG AA minimum. Target AAA on practice screens.

### Primary palette

| Token | Hex | RGB | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sky | `#4FB4E8` | 79, 180, 232 | Primary brand · CTAs · headers · links |
| Sunshine | `#FFCB4D` | 255, 203, 77 | Mascot · highlights · streaks |
| Coral | `#FF8A5C` | 255, 138, 92 | Celebration · achievement |
| Mint | `#6FD4B3` | 111, 212, 179 | Progress · growth |

### Sky ramp (tints, depth, dark mode)

| Stop | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| 50 | `#EBF6FD` | Lightest tint · backgrounds |
| 100 | `#C5E5F7` | Soft fill · pills |
| 200 | `#94D1F0` | Tint · hover states |
| 400 | `#4FB4E8` | **Primary brand** |
| 600 | `#1F73A8` | Borders · pressed states · darker accents |
| 800 | `#0E3A57` | Text on light Sky fills |
| 900 | `#072438` | Highest-contrast text |

### Neutrals

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#1A2541` | Primary text (warmer than pure black) |
| Slate | `#5A6378` | Secondary text |
| Mist | `#E2E8F0` | Borders, dividers |
| Cloud | `#F6F8FB` | Page background, surfaces |
| Pure | `#FFFFFF` | Cards |

### Semantic

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Success | `#34B475` | "Tone matched" · positive feedback |
| Warning | `#F5B642` | "Try once more" · gentle correction |
| Error | `#E85D5D` | **Technical errors only — never for pronunciation feedback** |
| Info | `#4FB4E8` | Hints, tips (same as primary Sky) |

### Color rules

- Red is for technical errors only. A mispronunciation uses Warning amber with kind copy. We do not tell a struggling speaker they are red.
- Always pair semantic color with an icon — never rely on color alone.
- Text on colored backgrounds: use the 800 or 900 stop from the same ramp, never plain black.

## 5. Typography

A three-font system. Rounded for warmth, clean for clarity, native for Cantonese.

### Font choices

| Role | Family | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Display | **Nunito** | Rounded terminals tested better with dyslexic readers; warm without being childish |
| Body | **Inter** | Industry-standard for screen readability; accessibility-tested at small sizes |
| Traditional Chinese | **Noto Sans HK** | Designed in HK, for HK; supports full HKSCS character set; free for commercial use |

All three are free via Google Fonts / fontsource — no licensing concerns.

### Type scale (1.25 ratio)

| Token | Size | Weight | Family | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | 48px | 500 | Nunito | Hero headlines |
| H2 | 32px | 500 | Nunito | Section headings |
| H3 | 22px | 500 | Nunito | Lesson titles, card headings |
| Body | 16px | 400 | Inter / Noto Sans HK | Paragraphs |
| Small | 13px | 400 | Inter / Noto Sans HK | Captions, metadata, helper text |
| Mono | 12px | 400 | system mono | Scores, timestamps, IPA |

### Typography rules

- Sentence case only. Never Title Case, never ALL CAPS.
- Two weights only: 400 regular, 500 medium. Never 600 or 700.
- Body line-height: 1.6 (English) / 1.7 (Chinese — characters need more breathing room)
- Display letter-spacing: -1% on H1, 0% elsewhere
- Mix English + Chinese freely; Chinese uses Noto Sans HK regardless of context

## 7. Voice & tone

### Tone shifts by surface

| Surface | Tone |
|---|---|
| Public marketing (`/for-schools`) | Calm, confident, results-aware |
| Public marketing (`/for-professionals`) | Practical, workflow-aware, peer-to-peer |
| Public marketing (`/for-families`) | Warm, reassuring, PiPi-led |
| Web app · practice screens | Calm, specific, encouraging |
| Web app · settings & admin | Neutral, informational |
| Crisis-adjacent copy (frustrated user, failing attempt) | Warmest and most spacious — short sentences, lots of breathing room |

### Do say / don't say

| Don't say | Do say |
|---|---|
| "Wrong. Try again." | "Nice — that second tone was clean. Ready to try once more?" |
| "You failed this exercise." | "Let's circle back to this one tomorrow." |
| "Fix your speech disorder." | "Build your speaking confidence." |
| "Patient" / "treatment" | "Learner" / "practice" |
| Generic "Good job!" | Specific: "Your tone shape on 媽 was bang on." |

### Vocabulary swap list

The brand explicitly avoids clinical/medic language. Across all surfaces, replace:

| Retire | Use instead |
|---|---|
| clinical-grade · clinical | everyday · at-home · daily |
| evidence-based · validated | designed for · built for · made for |
| therapy · treatment | practice · exercises · sessions |
| therapist · clinician | teacher · support staff · the people supporting you |
| patient · case | learner · user · child |
| communication disorder | speech needs · speaking confidence |
| diagnosis · assessment | progress tracking · attempt history |
| HKSAR-recognised · accredited | made in Hong Kong · for Hong Kong Cantonese |
| platform | assistant tool · web app · practice helper |

**Edge case — "speech professionals."** Still fine to mention, but never as "our partners" or "we work with." Phrase as: *"fits alongside the work of teachers and speech professionals,"* not *"endorsed by."* Until real partnerships are formalised, no implied endorsements.

## 8. Accessibility commitments

Our users include children with SEN, adults rebuilding speaking confidence, and parents navigating both. Accessibility is the starting point, not a polish pass.

| Commitment | Standard |
|---|---|
| Contrast | WCAG 2.2 AA minimum across all surfaces. Target AAA on practice screens. |
| Color independence | All semantic colors paired with icons or text. Never color alone. |
| Tap targets | 44 × 44px minimum. Generous spacing between interactive elements. |
| Bilingual default | Traditional Chinese (HK) and English available everywhere. |
| Motion | Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Animations are accents, not load-bearing. |
| Audio | No autoplay. All audio user-initiated. Captions/transcripts for any spoken content. |
| Streak pressure | Opt-in. No guilt language, no broken-streak punishment screens. |
| Keyboard | Full keyboard navigation. Visible 2px focus rings on Sky 600. |
| Screen reader | All icons either labelled or marked `aria-hidden`. Semantic HTML throughout. |

## 9. Launch context · pilot programme

### Timeline

- **June 2026** — Pilot begins, invite-only
- **June–August 2026** — Data collection through the pilot programme
- **Post-pilot** — Iterate based on real usage; broader rollout TBD

### Surface map at launch

| Surface | URL | Status at launch | Public-facing? |
|---|---|---|---|

| Web app | `speakable.naozumi.[domain]` | Live, invite-only | No — pilot participants & internal testing |

### What this means for the public site

- **No public pricing page** — pricing happens in demo conversations with schools
- **No self-serve sign-up** — single CTA is "Book a school demo" or "Apply to the pilot"
- **No "Sign in" link rendered prominently** — pilot users get a direct private URL
- **Trust bar uses pilot partners as they're confirmed** — placeholders fine pre-launch
- **Evidence/results section stays modest** — "Pilot insights to come" rather than premature claims
